Set up: Let's Start the Game

üéÆ

If you just want to play online Mahjong, please skip to Gameplay.

‚Üí

1 Determine the dealer

‚ñº

Dealer for the First Round: All players roll two dice, and the player with the highest sum of the number becomes the dealer (East). If there's a tie, re-roll the dice until a dealer is decided.

Dealer for the Next Round: After a game ends, the winner becomes the dealer for the next round. If there's a draw (no winner), the current dealer remains the dealer for the next round.

2 Seat Arrangement

‚ñº

The dealer sits in the east position, the remaining players take seats anticlockwise.

3 Shuffle & Build the Wall

‚ñº

All players shuffle the tiles face-down on the table. Then, each player stacks 34 tiles into a 2-tile-high "wall" (4 walls make a square).

4 Draw Starting Tiles

‚ñº

The dealer rolls two dice first. The total and the smaller number on the dice will decide the "starting position for drawing tiles" and the "number of reserved tile stacks":

  • Starting Position: Count anticlockwise from the dealer (the dealer counts as 1, the next player as 2, and so on) ‚Äî the player corresponding to the total dice count is the "starting postion".
  • Reserved Tile Stacks: Use the smaller number of the two dice (if both numbers are the same, use that number). From the left side of the wall in front of the starting position, the dealer reserves tile stacks equal to this number and draws tiles which starts from the next stack after the reserved ones.

Drawing Order & Quantity: East (the dealer) starts first, draw 4 tiles at a time, and the other players follow (clockwise). Do this for 3 rounds, and in the 4th round, each player takes 1 tile. When players have 13 tiles each, this starts the gameplay.

Gameplay: Draw, Discard, and Form Melds

The turn here goes anticlockwise (East ‚Üí South ‚Üí West ‚Üí North). If you are the dealer, on your turn:

Step 1: Draw a Tile

Take one tile from the wall, where the previous player left off.

Step 2: Check for a Win

If you form a winning hand, you may call "Mahjong!" and win immediately.

Step 3: Discard a Tile

If you don't win, choose 1 unwanted tile and discard it face-up. After discarding, there are two possibilities:

  • If no other players wish to "claim" the discarded tile, the turn proceeds to the next player (back to Step 1: Draw a Tile).
  • If any player wants to claim the discarded tile to form a Pung or Kong, they must declare it immediately ‚Äî the turn pauses to resolve the claim first.

Pung

01 Declaration: If the discarded tile is identical to any players' pair, the player can declare Pung.

02 Combination: Take that tile and combine it with their pair to form a set of three identical tiles.

03 Discard: Discard one tile from their hand. If no one claims the newly discarded tile, the turn passes to the player sitting anticlockwise next to the Pung declarer ‚Äî this player will return to Step 1: draw a tile.

Kong

01 Declaration: If the discarded tile is identical to any players' triplet, the player can declare Kong.

02 Combination: Take that tile and combine it with their triplet to form a set of four identical tiles.

03 Draw: Regardless of the type of Kong declared, the player must draw a tile from the end of the wall, and then check for a win.

04 Discard: If the player doesn't win, the player must discard one unwanted tile from their hand. If no one claims the newly discarded tile, the turn passes to the player sitting anticlockwise next to the Kong declarer ‚Äî this player will return to Step 1: draw a tile.

This is called an exposed Kong. There are two other types of Kong: concealed Kong, and added Kong.

Exposed Kong

When a player holds three identical tiles and another player discards the fourth matching tile.

+
Concealed Kong

When a player draws all four identical tiles on their own, either at the start of the game or over multiple turns.

Added Kong

When a player has already declared a Pung and later draws the fourth matching tile.

+
Note: After declaring a Pung or Kong, the involved tiles should be placed in the lower-right corner of the table, which can no longer be discarded and are not included to form a winning hand.
